Annual

Regular Maintenance: Schedule regular maintenance appointments with a trusted mechanic or dealership. This typically includes oil changes, filter replacements, fluid checks and top-ups, tire rotations, and brake inspections. Routine maintenance helps keep your vehicle's engine running smoothly, improves fuel efficiency, and extends the life of vital components. It also allows professionals to identify any potential issues early on and address them promptly, preventing expensive repairs in the future.

Vehicle

Exterior and Interior Cleaning: Regular cleaning of both the exterior and interior of your vehicle is essential to maintain its appearance and protect it from damage. Wash the exterior regularly to remove dirt, grime, and road salt, paying attention to the undercarriage and wheel wells. Waxing the vehicle's surface can provide additional protection against the elements. For the interior, vacuum carpets and seats, clean surfaces with appropriate products, and condition leather upholstery if applicable. Regular cleaning not only keeps your vehicle looking great but also helps preserve its value.

Care

Inspection and Testing: Conduct a thorough inspection of your vehicle's various components. Check the lights, including headlights, taillights, turn signals, and brake lights, to ensure they are functioning properly. Inspect the windshield wipers for any signs of wear and replace them if necessary. Test the battery's voltage and terminals, and consider having it tested professionally if it's been a while. Additionally, examine the tires for signs of wear and proper inflation. Uneven wear or insufficient tread depth may indicate the need for alignment, balancing, or tire replacement.
